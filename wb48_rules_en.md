# System WB-48 — Great Battles 1914-18
## Rules

> Extracted from 10 rulebook photos. Title page says "WB-95" but content is WWI (Tannenberg 1914). Section 5.1 confirms: "In WB-95 system (targeted for WB-48)". Rules shared between systems.

---

## 1.0 INTRODUCTION

The game simulates a battle fought in 1914 in East Prussia (Tannenberg/BURG 1914). Russian and German brigades and divisions fight on a hexagonal map.

---

### 2.1 Game Board
- Hexagonal map 100×70 cm
- Each hex represents approx. 7 km of real terrain
- Map reflects the characteristic terrain elements of the 1914 battleground
- Hexes numbered for identification; legend on map explains markings

## 3.0 PHASES AND STAGES

The game is played in stages (days), during which each side has the right or obligation to perform a series of actions. Stage tracking is done via the STAGE TRACK on the map and an ETAPY (stages) token.

To maintain order, all actions in each stage are divided into **12 phases**. Each phase has strictly defined actions. Performing any game action outside its designated phase is prohibited. A stage is considered complete after performing all actions in phases 1-12 in order. After completing the stage, advance the STAGE token one space, then begin phase 1 again.

**INITIATIVE** — a concept determining which side moves first in a given scenario.

### 3.1 Phases

| # | Phase | Description |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Player A's Air Phase | Player A may conduct independent air attacks on objects |
| 2 | Player A's Artillery Barrage | Player A may conduct artillery barrage if scenario permits (per 8.6) |
| 3 | Player A's Movement | Player A may move any number of units; may also bring reinforcements onto the board and build field fortifications (per 9.11) |
| 4 | Player A's Combat | Player A's units may attack enemy units; may support with artillery ("fire strength in attack"). During this phase Player B may support defense with own artillery ("fire strength in defense") |
| 5 | Player A's Strategic Movement | All Player A units that did not move in movement phase, did not build FF, and are covered with "Strategic Movement" token may conduct strategic movement (per 11.0) |
| 6 | Player A's Supply Phase | Player A checks supply of all own units on the board per section 12.0 |
| 7 | Player B's Air Phase | Same as phase 1, for Player B |
| 8 | Player B's Artillery Barrage | Same as phase 2, for Player B |
| 9 | Player B's Movement | Same as phase 3, for Player B |
| 10 | Player B's Combat | Same as phase 4, for Player B (Player A may support defense with artillery) |
| 11 | Player B's Strategic Movement | Same as phase 5, for Player B |
| 12 | Player B's Supply Phase | Same as phase 6, for Player B |

### 3.2 General Notes
Due to the identical structure of phases 1-6 and 7-12, for brevity: phases 1 and 7 are called "air phases", 2 and 8 "barrage phases", 3 and 9 "movement phases", 4 and 10 "combat phases", 5 and 11 "strategic movement phases", 6 and 12 "supply phases".

## 4.0 MOVEMENT RULES

### 4.1 General Rules
During movement phases (including strategic), players may move as many units as they deem appropriate, in any direction. Order of moving units is irrelevant. Units must move hex-by-hex in continuous movement. Skipping hexes is not allowed.

At the beginning of each own movement phase, all units have strictly defined Movement Point (MP) limits. Entering each subsequent hex costs MP; movement is possible until the unit expends its entire MP limit.

Movement is permitted only during: own movement phases, strategic movement phases, pursuit, and retreat.

### 4.2 Detailed Rules
- **4.21** A unit may not enter a hex occupied by an enemy unit.
- **4.22** A unit may not spend more MP in a given stage than its movement capability.
- **4.23** A unit need not expend its full MP limit in one stage.
- **4.24** Unused MP do not carry over to the next stage (do not accumulate).
- **4.25** MP may not be transferred between units.

### 4.3 Terrain Effects on Movement
- **4.31** The basic terrain type is clear terrain. Entry cost for clear terrain is given in the TERRAIN MOVEMENT COST table.
- **4.32** Entering a hex with terrain other than clear costs additional MP. The additional cost is defined in the TERRAIN MOVEMENT COST table.
- **4.33** Entry cost may differ for motorized units (tank icon on counter) vs non-motorized units (no tank icon) — per the TERRAIN MOVEMENT COST table.
- **4.34** A unit can ALWAYS move to an adjacent hex at the cost of ALL its MP available at the start of the movement phase (cannot enter inaccessible terrain).

> Example: An infantry unit has 3 MP. Entry cost for mountainous terrain with forest is 5 MP. If it's an adjacent hex, the unit can enter at the cost of all 3 MP.

### 4.4 Effect of Friendly Units on Movement
- **4.41** A unit may pass through a hex occupied by friendly units or their ZOC, incurring NO additional MP costs.
- **4.42** Any number of units may pass through a single hex during one movement phase.

## 5.0 STACKING

- **5.1** In the WB-48 system, after the movement phase ends, a single hex may contain units with a combined total of **6 Combat Proficiency Levels (CPL)**. HQ units count as 1 CPL. The order of units in a stack is irrelevant.

---

## 6.0 ZONE OF CONTROL (ZOC)

### 6.1 General Rules
- **6.11** A unit's Zone of Control (ZOC) consists of the six adjacent hexes surrounding the hex it occupies. Each unit controls six adjacent hexes.
- **6.12** All units (except artillery and HQs) project ZOC throughout the entire game.
- **6.13** Multiple friendly or enemy ZOCs on the same hex have no special interaction; all ZOCs function independently — they do not negate or exclude each other. Such a hex is considered controlled by multiple units.
- **6.14** ZOC does not extend across large rivers or into terrain inaccessible to the unit (e.g., swamps and mountains for tank units).
- **6.15** A hex is considered "controlled" by a player if the last unit to occupy it (or project ZOC onto it) belonged to that player.

### 6.2 Effect of ZOC on Movement
- **6.21** Upon entering an enemy unit's ZOC, a unit must IMMEDIATELY STOP regardless of remaining MP.
- **6.22** Entering enemy ZOC costs NO additional MP.
- **6.23** Moving one's own unit within an enemy unit's ZOC is only possible during pursuit.

### 6.3 Leaving ZOC
- **6.31** During own movement phase, a unit may leave enemy ZOC at a cost of 1 MP.
- **6.32** After leaving one enemy ZOC, a unit may re-enter the ZOC of the same or another enemy unit in the same movement phase. It may also move directly from one ZOC to another. However, it CANNOT move within one enemy unit's ZOC (i.e., from one ZOC hex to another ZOC hex of the same unit).

## 7.0 COMBAT

### 7.1 General Rules
- **7.11** Units may fight only during the combat phase. Combat between units is only possible when they are on adjacent hexes. The sole exception is artillery (see 8.0).
- **7.12** Attack resolution procedure (using the COMBAT RESULTS TABLE):
  - a) Attacker designates the attacking and defending units; decides whether to support with artillery — if yes, add artillery's fire strength to total attacking SP;
  - b) Defender may support defense with own artillery; similarly sums forces;
  - c) Compare attacker's and defender's strength, rounding DOWN (in defender's favor); ratios above 10:1 are treated as 10:1; below 1:4 as 1:4;
  - d) Apply combat modifiers (stars, fortifications, terrain, etc.). Note: attacker's modifiers applied first (up to 10:1 max), then defender's modifiers;
  - e) Find the appropriate column in the CRT;
  - f) Attacker rolls dice;
  - g) Read result at intersection of die roll row and force ratio column.

> Rounding examples: 3.2:1=3:1; 3.5:1=3:1; 3.6:1=4:1. Attacker strength 16 SP, defender 5 SP → 16:5=3.2:1 → rounds to 3:1.

### 7.2 Attack Requirements and Restrictions
- **7.21** All enemy units in the ZOC of the attacking player's units **MUST** be attacked.
- **7.22** All own units that have enemy units in their ZOC at the start of the combat phase **MUST** attack (exception: 9.23).
- **7.23** A unit may be attacked simultaneously by as many units as are in its ZOC (sum their SP). A single unit may attack multiple enemy units (if they are all in its ZOC) — defender sums SP of all defending units.
- **7.24** When multiple units defend on the same hex, they CANNOT be attacked separately. The attack must be resolved as a single combat, summing all defenders' SP.
- **7.25** When attacking units share the same hex, the attack MAY be split into separate attacks.
- **7.26** The attacker resolves attacks in any order.
- **7.27** Each unit may attack AND be attacked only ONCE per combat phase.
- **7.28** Attacks where multiple defending units are attacked simultaneously by multiple attacking units are FORBIDDEN, unless the defending or attacking units share a hex. Only simultaneous attacks from multiple hexes against ONE hex, or from one hex against multiple hexes, are allowed.
- **7.29** A unit cannot refuse combat. The combat phase continues until all mandatory attacks are resolved (fulfilling 7.21 and 7.22).
- **7.291** An attack against a standalone HQ is always resolved at the 10:1 column; regardless of result, the HQ is always eliminated.

### 7.3 Combat Effects
Symbols used in the COMBAT RESULTS TABLE:
- **A2-1** — Attacker loses 1 CPL and retreats 2 hexes; OR stays in place losing 3 CPL total. May also retreat 1 hex and lose 2 CPL.
- **A1-1** — Attacker loses 1 CPL and retreats 1 hex; OR stays in place losing 2 CPL total.
- **-1/1** — Both attacker and defender lose 1 CPL each.
- **-1/B1** — Attacker loses 1 CPL; defender retreats 1 hex OR loses 1 CPL staying in place.
- **B1** — Defender retreats 1 hex OR loses 1 CPL staying in place.
- **B2** — Defender retreats 2 hexes OR loses 2 CPL staying in place.
- **B3** — Defender retreats 3 hexes OR loses 3 CPL staying in place.
- **B4** — Defender retreats 4 hexes OR loses 4 CPL staying in place.
- **●** — Must roll for Disorganization (D).

> Partial retreat is possible: e.g. result B4 — retreat 2 hexes and lose 2 CPL.

- **7.32** Disorganization rolls:
  - Result A2, B2: 1 D roll
  - B3: 2 D rolls
  - B4, B5: 3 D rolls
  - For ● on attacker: 1 D roll
  - Note: If defender chose partial retreat, number of D rolls depends on actual retreat distance.
- **7.33** Retreat and losses apply to ALL attacking/defending units regardless of how many hexes they occupy.
- **7.34** A unit forced to retreat (result Ax, Bx) must retreat the indicated distance OR lose that many CPL (see 7.31). CPL loss is marked by flipping the counter to its back side (lower value).
- **7.35** CPL loss is IRREVERSIBLE.
- **7.36** The defender chooses which defending units take CPL losses and how many each.
- **7.37** A unit that loses 2 CPL (for artillery: 1 CPL) is ELIMINATED from the game.
- **7.38** If the attacker takes losses, the attacker chooses which attacking units lose CPL.
- **7.39** A retreating unit cannot enter enemy ZOC (exception: 7.42) or leave the board. If retreat is impossible, the unit is eliminated (staying in place and taking losses is preferable if possible).
- **7.40** Retreating units move regardless of MP; however, terrain accessibility must still be respected.
- **7.41** A unit cannot retreat across a large river (unless via a bridge).
- **7.42** A unit MAY retreat through friendly units. If retreat passes through enemy ZOC, the retreating unit/stack loses 1 CPL per hex of retreat in enemy ZOC.
- **7.43** A unit that retreated after combat cannot build field fortifications.
- **7.44** Retreat cannot end on a hex occupied by units with 6 total CPL (per stacking limit 5.1).

### 7.5 Pursuit
- **7.51** If the defeated unit chose to flee, ALL attacking units may pursue along the retreat path for the same number of hexes the defeated unit retreated.
- **7.52** If multiple defeated units retreated in different directions, pursuit may follow any retreat path.
- **7.53** A pursuing unit along the retreat path IGNORES enemy ZOC.
- **7.54** A pursuing unit must stop on the first hex that is NOT on the retreat path.
- **7.55** Pursuit ignores MP (but terrain accessibility is respected).
- **7.56** All victorious units may pursue EXCEPT artillery.
- **7.57** If the defeated unit was eliminated, the victor may enter its hex and move 1 hex in any direction (respecting terrain accessibility).
- **7.58** Pursuit across a large river: only the hex immediately across may be occupied. Only infantry may pursue across large rivers.

> Note: The next attack may only be resolved AFTER all effects of the previous attack (7.3, 7.5) have been applied.

### 7.6 Terrain Effects on Combat
- **7.61** Combat results depend only on the terrain the DEFENDER occupies. Attacker's terrain has no effect (exception: swamps — see TERRAIN CHARACTERISTICS table).
- **7.62** Terrain effects on combat are shown in the TERRAIN CHARACTERISTICS table. Numbers -1, -2 indicate column shifts LEFT (in defender's favor) on the CRT.
- **7.63** Terrain effects CUMULATE. E.g. forested hills = -1 (hills) + -1 (forest) = 2 columns left.
- **7.64** Small rivers affect combat when the MAJORITY of attacking SP attacks across the river. Large rivers HALVE the SP of attacking forces crossing them (round UP).
- **7.65** If multiple defenders in different terrains are attacked together, use the terrain of the strongest defending force; if equal, use the most favorable terrain for the defender.
- **7.66** Roads have no effect on combat results; only the underlying terrain matters.

## 8.0 ARTILLERY

### 8.1 General Rules
- **8.11** Artillery units are the ONLY units that can participate in combat without being in an enemy's ZOC. They may attack independently, support friendly attacks, and support friendly defense.
- **8.12** Field artillery units have only 1 CPL.
- **8.13** Artillery firing range for ALL artillery units: maximum **2 hexes**.
- **8.14** Field artillery cannot participate in pursuit or retreat.
- **8.15** Artillery units **do NOT project ZOC**.
- **8.16** An artillery unit is eliminated if during the movement phase or pursuit, an enemy unit with ZOC passes through the hex it occupies — the enemy unit spends 1 extra MP for this. If the artillery is entrenched (in FF), it costs 2 MP to overrun.

### 8.2 Independent Artillery Fire
- **8.21** Artillery may conduct independent fire only during its own combat phase, using the "fire strength in attack" value. Procedure:
  - a) Player declares artillery fire strength directed at a hex;
  - b) Player rolls 2 dice (2d6) to determine the result. Effect is found at the intersection of "die roll" column and "artillery SP limit" row in the ARTILLERY FIRE table.
- **8.22** An artillery unit may fire at only one hex (even if multiple units are on it); splitting fire across multiple hexes is forbidden.
- **8.23** Multiple artillery units may fire at the same hex (sum their fire strengths in attack).
- **8.24** The target unit must be within artillery range (max 2 hexes).
- **8.25** Artillery that was the TARGET of independent fire and survived may still support defense in the same combat phase.
- **8.26** Terrain on the target hex affects fire effectiveness. The terrain modifier (from TERRAIN CHARACTERISTICS table) is subtracted from the artillery SP used.

> Example: Artillery with 8 SP fires at a unit in forest. 8 SP - 1 (terrain modifier) = 7 effective SP.

### 8.3 Supporting Attack
- **8.31** Friendly attacks may be supported by one or more artillery units. Their "fire strength in attack" is added to the total attacking SP.
- **8.32** If artillery supports an attack against multiple enemy units, it is sufficient if at least one of them is within firing range.
- **8.33** The attacker must declare (before rolling dice) which artillery units support the attack.
- **8.34** Each artillery unit may support an attack only ONCE per combat phase.

### 8.4 Supporting Defense
- **8.41** Artillery may support defense of friendly units during the enemy's combat phase.
- **8.42** Artillery may support defense if at the start of the enemy combat phase it is NOT in enemy ZOC. If it is in enemy ZOC, it may only support units sharing its hex.
- **8.43** Supporting artillery adds its "fire strength in defense" to the defender's SP.
- **8.44** The unit being supported must be within the artillery's firing range.
- **8.45** Multiple artillery units may support defense of a single friendly unit (sum their fire strength in defense).
- **8.46** If multiple units are attacked, artillery support requires only one of them to be in range.
- **8.47** The defender declares artillery support ONLY AFTER the attacker reveals ALL forces participating in the attack (non-artillery + supporting artillery). The attacker may NOT subsequently adjust forces.
- **8.48** Each artillery unit may support defense only ONCE per combat phase.
- **8.49** Artillery supporting defense is NOT subject to combat results, EXCEPT if it shares a hex with the defending unit it supported.

### 8.5 Direct Combat (Attack and Defense)
- **8.51** An artillery unit in enemy ZOC at the start of the combat phase may only support combat of a unit sharing its hex. In attack: uses "fire strength in attack". In defense: uses "fire strength in defense", but only if sharing the hex with a non-artillery, non-HQ unit.
- **8.52** Artillery standing alone (or with only other artillery/HQ units) on a hex, when directly attacked, defends at 1 SP (entire stack).
- **8.53** In direct combat, artillery is subject to all combat results.

### 8.6 Artillery Barrage
- **8.61** If the scenario permits, a player may conduct an artillery barrage in phases 2 and 8. During a barrage, the strength of participating artillery units is **MULTIPLIED BY 2**. Units used in a barrage CANNOT: move in the next movement phase, support attacks in the next combat phase, OR support defense. At least **two artillery units** must participate in a barrage.

### 8.7 Artillery Movement
- **8.71** The front of an artillery counter shows a cannon icon (combat position); the back shows march position.
- **8.72** Artillery may ONLY move in march position (exception: 8.77).
- **8.73** Switching between combat and march position costs 1 MP.
- **8.74** After switching to march position, the unit may move using remaining MP.
- **8.75** Only in combat position may the unit participate in combat using its fire values (exception: 8.52).
- **8.76** All artillery moves as non-motorized units.
- **8.77** Any artillery unit may move 1 hex without leaving combat position.
- **8.78** Artillery forced to retreat after combat is **AUTOMATICALLY DESTROYED**.

## 9.0 FORTIFICATIONS

### 9.1 Field Fortifications (FF)
- **9.11** All units that during the current stage did NOT move during the movement phase AND were not forced to retreat in the last combat phase (taking losses is permitted) may build field fortifications during their fortification phase. Marked by placing an FF token under the unit.
- **9.12** FF may be built on any hex regardless of terrain, EXCEPT swamps.
- **9.13** The FF token is removed from the board if, after the movement phase ends, the hex is not occupied by any friendly unit.
- **9.14** It is possible for a unit to leave an FF hex during movement and another friendly unit to enter that hex in the same phase.
- **9.15** The FF token is removed if an enemy unit enters the hex. The same field fortifications cannot be used by both sides.
- **9.16** Destroyed FF may be rebuilt at the same location multiple times (per 9.11).
- **9.17** FF cannot be built on hexes containing permanent fortifications.

### 9.2 Effect of FF on Combat
- **9.21** When attacking a unit in FF, the result is looked up in a column shifted **2 left** (built-up area) or **1 left** (other terrain) from what the force ratio would indicate.
- **9.22** FF terrain effect cumulates with other terrain types. E.g. FF on hills = -1 (hills) + -1 (FF) = 2 column shift.
- **9.23** A unit in FF is **NOT obligated** to attack enemy units in its ZOC (this does NOT mean FF neutralize the enemy's ZOC).
- **9.24** Improved FF (red token) are given to players at scenario start. They cannot be built during the game. Subject to rules in 9.1.

### 9.3 Effect of FF on Movement
- **9.31** Entering or leaving a hex with FF incurs NO additional MP costs (only terrain costs apply).

### 9.4 Permanent Fortifications (PF)
PF are marked on the map as brown lines, or as single/connected hex fields.
- **9.41** PF HALVE the total SP of attacking forces if the attack passes through a PF-marked edge. If attacked from multiple sides: the modifier applies if the majority or half of attackers attack through the PF edge.
- **9.42** Fortresses are marked with red lines. Fortresses halve attacker's SP if attacking through the fortified edge AND apply a modifier of -2 (shift 2 columns left).
- **9.43** All units in PF are automatically considered fortified. If attacked from the rear, they receive a -1 defense modifier.
- **9.44** If the defender used PF in combat and the result is B1-B5, defending units may remain in place losing CPL instead:
  - B1 — no losses
  - B2 — lose 1 CPL
  - B3 — lose 2 CPL
  - B4 — lose 2 CPL
  - B5 — lose 3 CPL
- **9.45** If multiple units defend in PF, losses may be distributed among any of them.

## 10.0 HEADQUARTERS / COMMAND (HQ)

### 10.1 General Rules
The game includes tokens representing corps and army headquarters. They have significant influence on combat course and results.

### 10.2 Detailed Rules
- **10.21** HQ tokens do NOT project ZOC.
- **10.22** HQ movement capability: **8 MP**. HQs are treated as infantry formations.

### 10.3 Effect on Combat
- **10.31** All units within **2 hexes** of any own HQ gain combat support.
- **10.32** If ALL attacking units are within command range → shift CRT result 1 column in attacker's favor (RIGHT).
- **10.33** If ALL defending units are within command range → shift CRT result 1 column in defender's favor (LEFT).
- Note: If BOTH players account for HQ in combat, the effects CANCEL each other.
- **10.34** HQ combat effect does NOT stack — the presence of additional HQs provides no extra benefit.
- **10.35** HQs have 1 CPL.
- **10.36** HQs are not combat units; they cannot attack independently.
- **10.37** If a unit sharing a hex with an HQ suffers losses, the HQ may be eliminated instead.
- **10.38** An HQ is eliminated when an enemy unit with ZOC passes through or occupies its hex (e.g., during enemy movement phase or pursuit). Analogous to rule 8.16.

## 11.0 STRATEGIC MOVEMENT

### 11.1 General Rules
- **11.11** Strategic Movement (SM) in phases 5 and 11 serves to move own forces after the effects of own combat phase are known.
- **11.12** SM may be performed by any unit that: did NOT move in the last movement phase, did NOT fight, did NOT build field fortifications, is NOT in enemy ZOC, AND was covered with a "Strategic Movement" token during the movement phase.
- **11.13** SM is subject to all movement rules in section 4.0.

### 11.2 Detailed Rules
- **11.21** SM is limited only by available MP. A unit performing SM has its MP **REDUCED BY 2**.
- **11.22** During SM, a unit CANNOT enter enemy ZOC.
- **11.23** After completing SM, a unit CANNOT conduct any combat.

## 12.0 SUPPLY

### 12.1 General Rules
The fundamental factor for conducting military operations is the supply of fighting forces (ammunition, food, medicine, etc.). Its lack significantly affects unit mobility and combat capability.

### 12.2 Supply Line
- **12.21** A supply line is a continuous path of adjacent hexes from a supply base to a given unit.
- **12.22** Supply bases for each side are defined per scenario (typically a map edge, city, or supply base token).
- **12.23** A supply line may include unlimited hexes along roads or rail lines, but a LIMITED number of off-road hexes. Off-road capacity measured in MP:
  - Allies: 4 MP
  - Germans: 4 MP
  - Russians: 2 MP
  - MP costs for the supply line follow non-motorized movement costs.
- **12.24** A supply line cannot pass through a hex containing an enemy unit or through enemy ZOC, UNLESS a friendly unit occupies that hex.
- **12.25** For supply purposes, field artillery also projects ZOC. Additionally, "own" cities act like units (blocking enemy supply) but do not project ZOC.
- **12.26** Supply is only available to units reachable by a supply line.
- **12.27** Supply lines cannot pass through swamps, mountains, or large rivers.
- **12.28** Supply lines can be EXTENDED through own units. Each unit on the line extends it by 1 hex (no MP cost for that hex).

### 12.3 Supply Level
- **12.31** During the supply phase, supply is checked for all player's units. Units without supply receive a supply-lack token (level 1). In the next supply phase without supply → level 2, etc.
- **12.32** When a unit regains supply, the supply-lack token is removed immediately (during supply phase), restoring normal SP and MP regardless of how long it lacked supply.
- **12.33** Units with supply-lack level 2+ undergo a **SURRENDER TEST**. The player rolls a die when placing a level 2+ token. If the die roll is LESS THAN the supply-lack level number → the unit is eliminated (surrenders). A roll of 6 ALWAYS keeps the unit alive regardless of supply-lack level.

### 12.4 Effect of Supply on Combat and Movement
- **12.41** A unit without supply has its SP halved (round UP). E.g. SP 5 → 5÷2=2.5 → 3.
- **12.42** Affects both attack AND defense.
- **12.43** Artillery without supply: both "fire in attack" and "fire in defense" halved (round UP). E.g. fire in attack 5, fire in defense 3 → becomes 3 and 2.
- **12.44** Lack of supply reduces armored units' movement by 1/2.

### 12.5 Supply Bases (SB)
- **12.51** Number of Russian SB is defined per scenario.
- **12.52** Placement of SB is decided by the commanding player.
- **12.53** SB movement along rail lines: in Russia = 2 hexes per day; outside Russia = 1 hex per day.

## 13.0 BRIDGE DESTRUCTION & CONSTRUCTION

### 13.1 Bridges on Small Rivers
- **13.11** A bridge on a small river is destroyed if any non-artillery unit is on the hex with the bridge and the player declares destruction (cost: 1 MP). Place a "destroyed bridge" token on that edge.
- **13.12** A bridge on a small river is rebuilt if a non-artillery unit is on the hex and the player declares reconstruction (cost: 2 MP).
- **13.13** Bridges on small rivers can only be REBUILT at existing bridge locations, not built from scratch.

### 13.2 Bridges on Large Rivers
- **13.21** A bridge on a large river is destroyed analogously to 13.11 (cost: 2 MP).
- **13.22** Building/rebuilding a bridge on a large river: an HQ unit must be at the edge where the bridge is to be built. If the HQ stays for one full stage (not forced to flee), in the next stage (at the beginning of own movement phase) the destroyed bridge token may be removed or a "bridge" token placed. Bridges cannot be built in enemy ZOC.
- **13.23** A bridge may be destroyed by independent artillery fire or aviation (see OBJECT DESTRUCTION table).

## 14.0 DISORGANIZATION

- **14.1** A unit may become disorganized as a result of combat or independent artillery fire. A "D" token is placed on it.
- **14.2** A disorganized unit fights at **1/2 its SP** (round UP). Disorganization is automatically removed if the unit does NOT move and does NOT fight for one full day. The D token is removed at the end of the movement phase when conditions are met.
- **14.3** A disorganized unit **CANNOT** conduct pursuit.

## 15.0 AIRSHIPS

- An airship token may be placed on any hex during own movement phase. At that moment, roll a die:
  - 1-5: no effect
  - 6: airship destroyed
- If the airship is not destroyed, the hex it occupies does NOT provide supply for the enemy.

## 16.0 INITIAL DEPLOYMENT & REINFORCEMENTS

### 16.1 Reinforcements
- **16.11** During the game, each player receives reinforcements as new units. Timing and entry locations are in the REINFORCEMENT CALENDAR.
- **16.12** New units may enter the board at any point during the game, during their movement phase.
- **16.13** If the entry zone is occupied by the enemy or in enemy ZOC, apply rule 16.3.
- **16.14** Units may enter the board in stacks.
- **16.15** After entering the board, all rules in 4.0 and 6.2 apply.

### 16.3 Moving Reinforcements
- **16.31** If the reinforcement entry zone is in enemy ZOC, occupied by the enemy, or the player decides to change entry location → reinforcements may enter from an adjacent zone on the same board edge, using 1/2 MP.
- **16.32** If the player delays reinforcements for any time, they may enter from ANY point on the same board edge using full MP.
